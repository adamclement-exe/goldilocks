Welcome to Goldilocks: A Scrum Adventure!

Your goal is to help a team create the Goldilocks storybook using Scrum and Kanban principles over 3 Sprints.

Game Goal

  • Complete as many User Stories (parts of the book) as possible by moving them to the "Done" column.
  • Meet your chosen Definition of Done (DoD) by completing specific required stories by the end of Sprint 3 for bonus points.
  • Higher Value (‚≠ê) stories contribute more to the final "quality" of the storybook.

Game Flow: The Sprint Cycle (5 Days + Review/Retro)

Each Sprint follows this pattern:

1. Sprint Planning (Start of Sprint):
  • Review the Product Backlog.
  • Select User Stories for the Sprint Backlog based on your team's Capacity (total points they can likely complete).
  • Choose Implementation Options for stories if available (affects Effort & Outcome).
  • Action: Check story boxes, confirm choices, click "Commit to Sprint".
2. Day 1 Assignment:
  • Assign initial Developers (Text/Visual workers) to stories in the Sprint Backlog ('Ready' column).
  • You can assign multiple workers to a single story.
  • Assigning workers to a 'Ready' story moves it to 'In Progress', consuming a WIP (Work-In-Progress) Limit slot for that column. Be mindful not to exceed the limit!
  • Action: Use checkboxes to select workers for each story, then "Confirm Assignments".
3. Work Day 1 Simulation:
  • Workers automatically apply their Points Per Day (+ bonus for matching story tag) to their assigned stories.
  • Progress bars update. Stories finishing Development move to 'Testing'.
4. Daily Scrum & Reassignment (Days 2-5):
  • Review progress and any new Obstacles.
  • Unassign workers from stories (click the '‚úï' button).
  • Assign Additional Workers (Devs to 'In Progress', Testers to 'Testing') using the dropdowns. Respects WIP limits when adding the *first* tester to a full column.
  • Resolve Blocked Stories (üüß icon): Assign an available Senior Developer (costs 1 point from their daily total).
  • Action: Make assignments/unassignments, resolve blockers, click "Confirm Changes".
5. Work Day Simulation (Days 2-5):
  • Workers apply points to assigned, unblocked stories.
  • Stories finishing Development move to 'Testing'. Stories finishing Testing move to 'Done' (and workers are auto-unassigned).
6. Sprint Review (End of Sprint):
  • See completed stories, calculated Velocity (total points done), Value delivered, and Average Cycle Time.
  • View a preview of the storybook pages created.
  • Get Sponsor Feedback and DoD progress update.
  • Action: Click "Proceed to Retrospective".
7. Sprint Retrospective (End of Sprint):
  • Reflect on the sprint.
  • Action: Fill in the fields and click "Finish Retrospective" (or "Finish Game" after Sprint 3).

Key Concepts

  • User Stories: Have Effort (points), Value (‚≠ê), Tags (Visual, Text, Plot, etc.), Status (column), and sometimes Implementation Choices.
  • Kanban Board: Visualizes workflow. Stories flow from Ready -> In Progress -> Testing -> Done.
  • WIP Limits: Maximum number of stories allowed in 'In Progress' and 'Testing' simultaneously. Crucial for flow! Shown as (X/Y) on column headers. Assigning work that exceeds the limit will be blocked.
  • Multi-Assignment: You can assign multiple Devs to 'In Progress' stories or multiple Testers to 'Testing' stories to potentially finish them faster.
  • Team Workers: Have Area (Visual/Text/Testing), Skill (Senior/Junior), Points/Day. Seniors can unblock. Matching worker Area to story Tag gives a small work bonus.
  • Blockers & Obstacles: Random events. Blockers stop stories (need unblocking). Obstacles reduce worker points or availability. Check the Obstacle display in the Daily Scrum.
  • WIP Aging: How many days a story has been in its current column ('In Progress' or 'Testing'). High age (shown on card) indicates potential problems.
  • Cycle Time: Average time (in work days) a story takes from starting ('In Progress') to finishing ('Done'). Lower is generally better.

Tips for Success

  • Don't overcommit in Sprint Planning. Respect your team's capacity.
  • Pay close attention to WIP limits. Avoid starting too much work at once.
  • Use the Daily Scrum to reassign workers effectively if stories get blocked or priorities change.
  • Assign Senior Devs to unblock critical stories quickly.
  • Consider assigning multiple workers to high-priority or high-effort stories.
  • Balance completing stories (Velocity) with delivering valuable features (Value ‚≠ê) and meeting your DoD.

Scrum & Kanban Basics

Key Concepts

  • Visualize Workflow: Use the Kanban board to see work move through stages (Ready, In Progress, Testing, Done).
  • Limit Work-In-Progress (WIP): Set maximums for 'In Progress' and 'Testing' columns. This prevents bottlenecks, improves focus, and encourages finishing work ('Stop Starting, Start Finishing'). Exceeding WIP limits often slows down the entire system.
  • Manage Flow: Aim for a smooth, steady flow of work through the board. Identify and address bottlenecks (where work piles up). Track metrics like Cycle Time.
  • Make Policies Explicit: Understand the rules (like WIP limits, how blockers are handled, Definition of Done).
  • Implement Feedback Loops: Use Daily Scrums, Sprint Reviews, and Retrospectives to inspect and adapt.
  • Improve Collaboratively: Use feedback to make changes for the next Sprint.

Game Specifics

  • WIP Limits: Displayed on column headers (e.g., "WIP: 1/5"). Assigning work will be blocked if a limit is reached when moving a story into a column.
  • WIP Aging: Shows how many days a card has been in 'In Progress' or 'Testing' (e.g., "Age: 2d"). High age might indicate a problem or bottleneck.
  • Cycle Time: Calculated in Sprint Review. Measures the average time (in work days) from starting development ('In Progress') to finishing testing ('Done'). Lower is generally better.
  • Blockers: Obstacles can block stories (indicated by üüß border/icon). Blocked stories cannot progress. Assign a Senior Developer during the Reassignment phase to unblock a story (costs 1 pt!).
  • Multi-Assignment: You can assign multiple workers (Devs or Testers) to the same story during the Assignment/Reassignment phases to speed up progress.
